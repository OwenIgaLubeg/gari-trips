Uganda’s Best Wildlife and Waterfall Safari Destination

Murchison Falls National Park

Murchison Falls National Park is Uganda’s largest and oldest national park, offering one of the most dramatic and diverse safari experiences in East Africa. Located in northwestern Uganda, it’s named after the mighty Murchison Falls, where the Nile River explodes through a narrow 7-meter gorge and plunges 43 meters below. This iconic park is ideal for travelers searching for a Big Five safari in Uganda, combining spectacular wildlife encounters with powerful natural scenery.

Why Visit Murchison Falls National Park?

  • Most powerful waterfall in the world, the dramatic Murchison Falls.
  • One of the best places for Big Game Safaris in Uganda.
  • Rich diversity of wildlife: lions, elephants, giraffes, leopards, buffaloes, and hippos.
  • Both savannah and river ecosystems – unique for varied safari activities.
  • Proximity to Budongo Forest, a hotspot for chimpanzee tracking.

Top Activities in Murchison Falls Park

  • Game Drives – Explore the Buligi, Albert, and Queen’s Tracks for lions, elephants, giraffes, and more.
  • Boat Safari on the Nile – Cruise to the base of Murchison Falls to spot hippos, crocodiles, elephants, and birdlife.
  • Hike to the Top of the Falls – A thrilling climb with spectacular views of the Nile exploding through the gorge.
  • Chimpanzee Tracking – In nearby Budongo Forest, one of the best primate destinations in Uganda.
  • Bird Watching – Over 450 species, including the rare shoebill stork, goliath heron, and African fish eagle.
  • Sunset and Nature Walks – Especially rewarding along the riverbanks and savannah edges.
  • Photography Safaris – Incredible light, wildlife, and unspoiled scenery ideal for professional photographers.

Accommodation in Murchison Falls Park.

Whether you’re looking for a luxury safari lodge in Uganda or budget safar:

Popular accommodation facilities include:

  • Luxury: Nile Safari Lodge, Chobe Safari Lodge, Baker’s Lodge.
  • Midrange: Murchison River Lodge, Pakuba Safari Lodge.
  • Budget: Red Chilli Rest Camp, Fort Murchison Lodge.

Best Time to Visit Murchison Falls Park.

  • December to February and June to August – dry seasons are best for game drives and boat cruises.
  • Wildlife is easier to spot as animals gather around water sources.
  • The falls are impressive all year round, but boat trips are smoother during dry months.

How to Get to Murchison Falls Park.

  • By Road: Approx. 5–6 hours from Kampala via Masindi (most used route).
  • By Air: Daily flights from Entebbe to Pakuba, Chobe, or Bugungu Airstrips (30–45 mins).

Frequently Asked Questions

  • 1. Is it safe to visit Murchison Falls National Park?

    Yes, the park is generally safe for tourists, especially when traveling with a reputable tour company. However, it's always wise to follow safety guidelines provided by your guides.

  • 2. What should I pack for my safari?

    It’s recommended to pack lightweight clothing, a hat, sunscreen, insect repellent, binoculars, and a camera to capture the stunning scenery and wildlife.

  • 3. Is the Murchison Falls National Park biggest game park in Uganda?

    Yes, the park is biggest in the country and it is highly guarded by the government.

  • 4. What wildlife can I expect to see on a safari?

    Visitors can see a variety of wildlife, including elephants, lions, giraffes, hippos, and numerous bird species, making it a great destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

  • 5. What are the park entrance fees?

    Entrance fees vary based on nationality and age, so it's advisable to check the latest rates before your visit.
