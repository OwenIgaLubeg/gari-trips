Chimpanzee Tracking Capital of Uganda

Kibale National Park

Kibale National Park is Uganda’s premier destination for chimpanzee tracking safaris and primate walks. Located in western Uganda near Fort Portal, this tropical rainforest is home to the highest density and variety of primates in East Africa, including over 1,500 chimpanzees. Its beautiful jungle trails, rich birdlife, and connection to nearby crater lakes make it a must-visit for tourists seeking eco safaris in Uganda or a primate-focused wildlife adventure.

Why Visit Kibale National Park?

  • One of the best places in Africa for chimpanzee tracking.
  • Home to 13 primate species including red colobus, L’Hoest’s monkey & black and-white colobus.
  • Rich biodiversity: 70+ mammals, 370+ bird species, and stunning rainforest flora.
  • Easy access from major safari circuits: close to Queen Elizabeth and Semuliki National Parks.
  • Ideal for short Uganda safaris, family nature tours, and birding trips.

Top Activities in Kibale National Park

  • Chimpanzee Tracking – Track habituated chimp groups with expert guides in the forest.
  • Chimpanzee Habituation Experience (CHEX) – Spend quality unlimited time following chimps from nest to nest.
  • Bird Watching – Spot the African pitta, green-breasted pitta, and yellow spotted nicator.
  • Guided Forest Walks – Explore towering trees, butterflies, and elusive mammals.
  • Crater Lake Hikes & Community Tours – Combine nature and culture in the scenic Ndali-Kasenda Crater Region.
  • Nocturnal Walks – Look out for bush babies, nightjars, and tree hyrax after dark.
  • Photography Safaris – Incredible light, wildlife, and unspoiled scenery ideal for professional photographers.

Best Time to Visit Kibale National Park.

  • December to February and June to September are the best months for chimp tracking and forest hikes.
  • Rainy months (March–May & October–November) bring lush scenery and fewer tourists but can be muddy.

How to Get to Kibale National Park.

  • By Road: Approx. 5–6 hours from Kampala/Entebbe via Fort Portal.
  • By Air: Flights from Entebbe to Kasese Airstrip, then 1.5–2 hours drive to Kibale.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • 1. How do I obtain a chimpanzee tracking permit?

    Chimpanzee tracking permits can be purchased through the Uganda Wildlife Authority or authorized tour operators. It is advisable to book in advance, as permits are limited and can sell out quickly.

  • 2. Are there any cultural experiences available near Kibale National Park?

    Yes, visitors can engage in cultural experiences with local communities, including visits to traditional villages, learning about local customs, and participating in community projects and activities.

  • 3. Can I see other primates besides chimpanzees in Kibale National Park?

    Yes, Kibale is home to a variety of primate species, including olive baboons, grey-cheeked mangabeys, and several species of monkeys, making it a great destination for primate lovers.

  • 4. Do I have to buy a ticket for my infant?

    Yes, infants typically require a ticket, but it's best to check with the specific tour operator for their policy.

  • 5. What should I pack for a visit to Kibale National Park?

    It’s recommended to pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sturdy hiking boots, a hat, sunscreen, insect repellent, binoculars, a camera, and a rain jacket due to the park's unpredictable weather.
